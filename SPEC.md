## 1. Problem & user

**Pain.** A Bauleiter or Bauträger in DACH operates against a regulatory perimeter that changes constantly — Bebauungspläne, Bauordnung-Amendments, DIN-Norm updates, Bauausschuss decisions, Auslegungs-Verfahren — published across fragmented PDFs, Amtsblätter, and Ratsinformationssysteme. Large players (Strabag, Hochtief, Bilfinger) employ floors of staff lawyers and permit expediters to track this. Solo contractors and small Bauunternehmen cannot — they swing hammers all day and read Amtsblätter at midnight.

**The Bestandsschutz objection — and why the product still matters.** German construction law protects already-permitted in-progress projects via Bestandsschutz (Art. 14 GG; BVerfGE 35, 263), Vertrauensschutz (§ 48/49 VwVfG), Verhältnismäßigkeitsprinzip (Art. 20 III GG), and Übergangsregelungen. But:

- Bestandsschutz is a *defense*. You cannot invoke a defense you don't know about.
- 40–60% of a typical Bauträger's portfolio is in a lifecycle stage where current rules still apply (pre-Antrag, Antrag eingereicht, Umbau/Erweiterung planning).
- The architect who signed the Bauantrag has ongoing professional liability under HOAI regardless of the Bauherr's Bestandsschutz.
- Insurance and financing require *documented* vigilance — not just legal protection.
- *Offensive* use cases (Auslegung deadlines for objecting to neighboring projects) sit entirely outside the Bestandsschutz frame.

The product is **regulatory intelligence**, not compliance enforcement. Compliance is the floor; intelligence is the value.

**Primary user (v0.1 hackathon wedge).** Solo / small-shop Bauleiter or small Bauunternehmen, 1–10 active projects, no in-house legal, no compliance officer. DACH-region. Native DE speaker. Operates in Sie/Ihr-Form professional register. Mannheim-region for the demo.

**Job to be done.** *"Tell me — every morning, before I start work — what changed at any Bauamt in my catchment that actually matters for one of my projects, in the lifecycle stage it's in. Then give me a draft I can send to my architect."*

**XPRIZE Category 3 narrative.** Give the one-truck contractor the administrative early-warning system that Strabag maintains with 40 staff lawyers. Globally: 100,000 small contractors avoid the permit-misalignment tax.

## 2. Product ladder (T0–T4)

Pricing tiers gated by data trust, not feature count. Each tier asks for more customer data and pays more. The hackathon ships T0 as the volume revenue layer; T2 appears in the live judging demo.

| Tier | Price | Customer shares | Customer gets |
|------|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| **T0 — Bauamt-Alerts** (v0.1, hackathon wedge) | €29/mo | Gemeinde + Flurstück only | Plain alerts: "something happened in your Gemeinde affecting your Flurstück" |
| **T1 — Project-Aware** | €149/mo | Active project metadata (address, Bauantrag-Nr, type, timeline, **lifecycle stage**) | Alerts referenced to their specific project, lifecycle-aware suppression of inapplicable changes |
| **T2 — Blueprint-Integrated** | €499/mo | Blueprints (PDF) | Geometry-aware analysis ("western wall now 0.5m too close"), drafted Addendum |
| **T3 — Full Project Partner** | €2–5K/mo | Workflow + timeline + cost data | Financial-impact estimates, architect routing, neighbor-objection heat-map, pre-flight Antrag check |
| **T4 — On-Prem Bauträger** | €5–15K/mo + €15–40K setup | Historical Bauanträge corpus | Gemma 4 LoRA-fine-tuned on customer's writing, air-gapped, "blueprints never leave your network" |

**Tier-progression invariant:** customer trust escalates with track record. T0 has zero data exposure for the customer. Each upgrade unlocks deeper value in exchange for deeper data sharing.

## 3. Output shapes per tier

### T0 output (the hackathon revenue layer)

Daily email to the subscriber's inbox at 06:00 local, generated by the 03:00 cron.

- **Subject:** `[PermitWatchDog] {Datum} — {N} Bauamt-Änderung(en) in {Gemeinde}`
- **Body:** structured list of new filings in the subscriber's Gemeinde that mention their Flurstück OR fall within their watch-radius. Each filing summarized in one paragraph DE Sie-Form with a source URL.
- **Heartbeat:** if zero filings, send a one-line `[PermitWatchDog] Heute keine Bauamt-Änderungen` so silence is distinguishable from a broken cron.
- No drafted Addendum, no architect routing, no blueprint cross-reference.

### T2+ output (the judging-demo layer and beyond)

Per-project Outlook draft to the architect, contractor cc'd. Same shape as v0.1 SPEC:

- **Subject:** `[Permit-Alert] {Projektname} — {Kurzbeschreibung}`
- **Body (DE, Sie/Ihr-Form, ≤250 words, ≤4 paragraphs):**
  1. *Was hat sich geändert.* One sentence + source URL.
  2. *Wie betrifft es dieses Projekt — und welche rechtliche Position gilt.* Cross-referenced to project facts, explicit statement of legal stance: *"Aufgrund Bestandsschutz besteht kein zwingender Handlungsbedarf, jedoch …"* / *"Da sich Ihr Antrag noch im Genehmigungsverfahren befindet, gilt die neue Regelung."*
  3. *Bauantrags-Addendum (Entwurf).* 3–5 sentences in formal DE Bauwesen-Sprache.
  4. *Bitte um Stellungnahme.* Action + deadline.
- **Attachment:** PDF Addendum draft, A4, footer `Entwurf — KI-generiert — vor Versand prüfen`.
- **Risk-flag metadata:** `LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / NO_IMPACT_BESTANDSSCHUTZ / NO_IMPACT_OTHER`. The `NO_IMPACT_BESTANDSSCHUTZ` and `NO_IMPACT_OTHER` cases do NOT generate an email — they appear in the weekly digest only.

### Approval flow (all tiers above T0)

The drafted email lands in the contractor's Outlook *drafts*, never sent automatically. Contractor reviews at 06:30 and clicks send. Human-in-the-loop is the feature.

## 4. Inputs & sources

**Per user (one-time at onboarding, updatable):**
- T0: Gemeinde + Flurstück list (or watch-radius polygon).
- T1+: per project: address, Flurstück, Bauantrag-Nr, Gemeinde, project type (Wohnungsbau / Gewerbe / Tiefbau / Umbau / Sanierung), architect contact, planned-completion date, **lifecycle stage** ∈ {pre-Antrag, Antrag eingereicht, Antrag genehmigt, im Bau, Rohbau, Ausbau, abgenommen}.
- T2+: project blueprints (PDF).
- T4: historical Bauanträge corpus (PDF + structured).

**Per Gemeinde, scraped/ingested nightly between 02:00 and 03:00 local:**
- Bauamt website (Mannheim demo target: `stadt.mannheim.de/bauen`). WebMCP if published (Phase 3 ambition); otherwise headless Chromium in Antigravity managed Linux sandbox.
- Amtsblatt der Stadt Mannheim — weekly PDF.
- Ratsinformationssystem.
- Bauausschuss-Sitzungsprotokolle — monthly PDF.
- (Layered redundancy for reliability — see `permitwatchdog_strategy.md`.)

**Reasoning models:**
- **Orchestration agent:** Gemini 2.5 Pro on Antigravity 2.0.
- **Subagents:** Scraping, Filing-Parser, Cross-Reference, Relevance-Scorer, Drafter, Quality-Gate, Notifier.
- **Enterprise T4:** Gemma 4 LoRA-fine-tuned on customer's historical Bauanträge, deployed on-prem.

### Operational constraints
- **Latency:** daily run must finish — drafts in Outlook / alerts in inbox — before **06:00 local**. 3-hour budget from 03:00 cron.
- **Cost ceiling at T0:** ≤ €0.50/user/month in API + infra (gross margin >98%). Above this the €29/mo tier breaks.
- **Accuracy floor:** **No false-positive alert may reach the architect** for T2+. Every email leaves through the contractor's hand. Silent miss for v0.1 is acceptable, false alarm to architect is not.
- **Legal-correctness floor:** alerts MUST factor `lifecycle_stage` against the four-layer DACH doctrine (Bestandsschutz / Vertrauensschutz / Verhältnismäßigkeit / Übergangsregelung). Suppressing inapplicable changes is *correct behavior*, not silent failure.
- **Language:** product outputs in German, Sie/Ihr-Form. Judge-facing artifacts (submission video, deck) in English.
- **Data sovereignty:** T0–T3 hosted DE-only. T4 on-prem, customer-network only.

## 7. Edge cases (selected — full list in EVALS.md)

- **Bestandsschutz applies → suppress.** If `lifecycle_stage ∈ {im Bau, Rohbau, Ausbau, abgenommen}` AND the change does not invoke Gefahrenabwehr/Rücknahme/new-Antrag exception, flag `NO_IMPACT_BESTANDSSCHUTZ` and route to weekly digest only. No architect email.
- **Vertrauensschutz applies but partial exposure** (e.g., Antrag genehmigt, work not begun, customer considering Umbau). Flag `MEDIUM` with explicit legal-stance paragraph in body. Do NOT auto-suppress.
- **New Antrag pipeline.** If customer has any project at `lifecycle_stage ∈ {pre-Antrag, Antrag eingereicht}`, current-rule changes always apply. Flag according to severity.
- **Auslegung offensive use case.** If a *neighboring* project enters its 30-day public-review window, alert the customer regardless of their own Bestandsschutz state.
- **Bauamt site down at 03:00** — retry every 30 min until 05:30. After that, skip-with-log + send heartbeat "Scraper-Ausfall — Daten von gestern" rather than silently fail.
- **PDF Amtsblatt is image-scanned** — Gemini multimodal OCR. If page confidence < 0.80, flag for next-day human review.
- **Filing's Flurstück matches none of the customer's projects** — ingest to moat-data archive, suppress from user alerts.
- **Two customer projects affected by one filing** — emit one email per project (different architects, different facts).
- **Same filing referenced in Amtsblatt + Bauamt site + Bauausschuss-Protokoll** — deduplicate on Bauantrag-Nr.
- **No filings today** — heartbeat line so silence is distinguishable from broken cron.

## 9. Technical architecture (9-step, see `permitwatchdog_tech.md` for full detail)

1. **Onboarding** — customer + projects + lifecycle stage.
2. **Project Index** — Gemini multimodal extracts plan facts from uploaded blueprints (T2+); stored in Cloud SQL.
3. **Daily Scraping** centralized 02:00–03:00 — multi-source per Gemeinde.
4. **Filing Parsing** — Gemini Flash extraction to structured records.
5. **Cross-Reference Engine** — Flurstück / radius / Paragraph / Bebauungsplan-Bereich matching.
6. **Relevance Scoring** — Gemini Pro reads filing + project profile + `lifecycle_stage`; factors four-layer DACH doctrine; outputs risk flag + impact + Addendum draft.
7. **Quality Gate (E4b)** — automated rubric (6 dimensions); blocks below threshold.
8. **Delivery** — Outlook Graph API draft + heartbeat handling.
9. **Feedback loop** — thumbs-up/down → relevance-weight updates.

### WebMCP integration

PermitWatchDog ships as a **WebMCP provider** at launch:
- `mcp.permitwatchdog.com` exposing public tools (`lookup_filing_by_address`, `get_alerts_for_flurstueck`, `get_jurisdiction_coverage_status`) and authenticated subscriber tools (`get_my_projects`, `get_my_alerts`, `request_addendum_draft`).
- `llm.txt` at `permitwatchdog.com/llm.txt` defining agent terms-of-use.
- ARIA-tagged dashboard for agent navigability.

Ingestion layer also architected as **WebMCP consumer** (stub-ready; activated when Bauämter publish endpoints).
